Flex Week 11 — Revision and unit close

The final week: exam revision, a gallery walk of the unit’s best work, and a grateful close. Everything on this page can be taught as-is — the revision booklet slots in when it is ready.

Exam revision — resources coming soon

In preparation: the exam revision booklet (L29) is being built once last year’s exam is available to align it against. It will slot in here without changing anything else on this page.

Revising well in the meantime

  • The capacity matrix leads. Students revised honestly in Flex Week 10 (L28); their two goal rows tell each of them exactly where to start. Open the lesson by having students re-read their goal-setting box.
  • Best revision materials already exist: each student’s completed worksheets, the 12 key terms from the vocabulary blitz, and the graphing tasks (line, column and sector graphs) they constructed during the unit.
  • Quick structures that work with no booklet: re-run the vocabulary blitz from L28_PPT.pptx; pairs re-do one graph from a unit data table; students write one describe–explain–judge paragraph on a strategy they did not choose in Week 9.
  • Remind students: the exam covers the whole unit, and their matrix is their personal revision map.

Gallery and reflection lesson

Purpose: students see the sweep of what they made and learnt across the unit — and name how they have changed.

Lesson outline

  1. Set up (before class): display the unit’s best work around the room — trade-flow maps, supply-chain diagrams and the PSAs from L27. Desks to the walls if possible; the room becomes a gallery.
  2. Gallery walk (15 min): students move in silence or low voices with a sticky note pad. At each display: one specific compliment (“clear arrows on the trade flows”, not “nice”) left beside the work.
  3. Reflection (10 min): silent writing against the three prompts below.
  4. Share (10 min): volunteers read one reflection sentence aloud; teacher affirms the range — different students grew in different places.
  5. Unit close: the gratitude worldview moment (below) ends the unit.
Reflection prompts:
  • The most interesting thing I learned…
  • I got better at…
  • One way my choices changed…

Notes for the teacher

  • Every student should find at least one piece of their own work on display — check coverage when setting up.
  • Model a specific compliment first; ban “good” and “nice” on sticky notes.
  • Reflections make excellent evidence of learning growth — collect them if useful, but let students keep a copy.

Unit close — a grateful finish

Christian worldview — gratitude (1 Thessalonians 5:18): “Give thanks in all circumstances.” The unit ends where the topic test ended — in gratitude: for a world woven together in ways we now see more clearly, for the unseen hands behind everything we use, and for a term of honest effort. Whatever the exam brings, a student’s worth is not a mark. Close by thanking the class, specifically, for the term.

This close takes five minutes and is worth protecting — it is the last word of the unit.
