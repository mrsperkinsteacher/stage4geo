Flex Week 10 — From Knowing to Doing

Consolidation week: students turn nine weeks of learning into one realistic action and a public message (L27), then take an honest stocktake of everything they know with a capacity matrix (L28). Thread: Seeking Justice / Caretaking Earth.

Flex Week — this week doubles as catch-up time

If lessons were lost earlier in the term, use this week to recover them. Priority order:

  1. Inquiry checkpoint (L21)
  2. Topic test
  3. L28 capacity matrix
  4. L27 PSA

The two lessons below stand alone and need no new content knowledge — either can be dropped or delayed without breaking anything else.

Lesson 27 — My ethical action plan (PSA)

Learning intention: We are learning to turn what we know about trade into one realistic action and a clear public message (a PSA).

Lesson outline

  1. Starter: “I used to think… now I think…” thinking routine on the whole unit — 4 minutes silent writing in worksheet Task 1, then a pair share. Listen for genuine shifts.
  2. Recap: the four action options students already know from the unit — buy less / buy second-hand, check labels and origins, choose certified products (Fairtrade, RSPO), tell others what you know.
  3. Teach: what makes a good PSA — one clear message, a named audience, a call to action, one accurate fact — followed by a worked example plan on the slide.
  4. Worldview: James 2:14–17 — faith without deeds (see below).
  5. Main task: worksheet — action plan scaffold (Task 2), PSA planning frame with sketch box (Task 3), then students create the PSA as a poster by hand or digitally.
  6. Exit ticket: my one realistic change / the first person I’ll tell / which lesson my fact came from.

Activity instructions

  • Accuracy rule (enforce it): facts on the PSA may only come from this unit’s lessons, and students record which lesson each fact came from. Their completed worksheets are the approved fact bank — no half-remembered internet statistics.
  • Insist on the order: plan → teacher check → poster. A checked plan makes the poster quick and good.
  • Hand-drawn and digital posters are equally valid — say “a poster tool of your choice” for digital.
  • Support: sentence starters and a fact-bank pointer are in the worksheet SUPPORT box. Extension: add a second fact from a different lesson, and write one sign the PSA worked.
  • Collect or photograph finished posters — they are the centrepiece of the Flex Week 11 gallery lesson.
Christian worldview — Seeking Justice (James 2:14–17): “Faith by itself, if it is not accompanied by action, is dead.” Knowledge about injustice invites a response — not to fix everything, but to do one real thing. Discussion: “What’s the difference between feeling bad about something and doing something?”

Lesson 28 — Capacity matrix review

Learning intention: We are honestly judging what we know and can do across the whole unit, and setting goals for what to strengthen.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter: vocabulary blitz — the unit’s 12 key terms matched to definitions, closed book, 5 minutes; answers revealed and peer-marked.
  2. Teach: the four capacity levels — Information (I’ve heard of it), Knowledge (I can explain it), Know-how (I can do/apply it), Wisdom (I could teach it and judge with it) — then a worked example row modelling honest self-assessment with evidence.
  3. Main task: students complete the 14-row capacity matrix silently (about 15 minutes) with their worksheets and books open beside them as evidence.
  4. Class review game: teacher chooses one — quiz game with the 12 terms, concept-map race, or student-written quiz questions traded between pairs.
  5. Worldview: Philippians 1:9 (see below).
  6. Exit ticket: the goal-setting box at the end of the matrix — two rows to strengthen before the exam, and the plan.

Activity instructions

  • The evidence column is the engine of the lesson: naming a real artefact (a worksheet, a graph, a paragraph) forces students back through their own work, which is itself revision.
  • Circulate during the matrix and gently challenge suspicious columns of Wisdom ticks — “show me the evidence for row 7”.
  • Ticking a low level is framed as useful information, not failure. The matrix is for the student, not for marks — collect nothing.
  • All three game options need no technology. The concept-map race needs the 12 terms cut onto slips; every arrow drawn must be explained aloud.
  • Students keep the completed matrix — it is their personal revision map and returns in Flex Week 11.
Christian worldview — growth in love and knowledge (Philippians 1:9): “That your love may abound more and more in knowledge and depth of insight.” Learning is growth in love with knowledge — reviewing the unit isn’t just exam preparation. Discussion: “How could knowing more about how the world trades help you care for people better?”
