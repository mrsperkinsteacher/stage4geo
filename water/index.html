Water in the World

A 10-week unit on water as a resource: its distribution, the water cycle, scarcity, contamination, its value to different cultures, the water hidden in food and clothing, and flooding as a water hazard.

Water in the World examines water as a renewable resource: how it moves through the environment, why it is so unevenly available, how communities value and manage it, and what happens when there is too little of it — or, as in the 2022 Lismore floods, far too much.

Why is water available in some places and not others — and whose job is it to do something about it?
