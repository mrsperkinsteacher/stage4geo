Week 6 — Contaminated Water

Syllabus focus: Water in the World. Outcomes: GE4-MAN-01, GE4-TAP-01. Thread: Seeking Justice.

Week at a glance: L16 covers the causes and diseases linked to contaminated water; L17 is a short case study; L18 applies PQE to maps of unsafe water access.
LESSON 16

Causes and Diseases

Learning intention: we are learning what causes water contamination and the diseases it leads to.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think happens to water once it's been used and not treated properly?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Teach the causes (sewage/industrial waste, agricultural runoff, dumping, poor sanitation) and the diseases (cholera, dysentery, typhoid), including that around 2 billion people drink water contaminated by faeces.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Amos 5:24 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Build a fact file on each disease: cause, symptoms, prevention.
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one way a community can prevent cholera from spreading.

Main task instructions

  1. Copy the four causes of contaminated water with a one-line explanation each.
  2. Research and complete a fact file for cholera, dysentery and typhoid (cause, symptoms, prevention).
  3. Answer: why do these diseases spread fastest where sanitation infrastructure is weakest?

Christian worldview — Seeking Justice

“Let justice roll on like a river, righteousness like a never-failing stream.”

Amos 5:24

The imagery of a flowing river is used for justice itself — worth remembering when studying who does, and doesn’t, have a never-failing stream of clean water.

LESSON 17

Case Study: Unsafe Water and Health

Learning intention: we are learning how a lack of access to safe water affects a densely populated place.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What might daily life look like without a safe, reliable water supply at home?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Short case study of a densely populated area with limited access to safe water.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Summarise the causes, impacts and any local solutions attempted in the case study area.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one local solution attempted in the case study, and whether you think it would work elsewhere.

Main task instructions

  1. Read the case study.
  2. Summarise its causes, impacts and any solutions attempted, under three headings.
  3. Answer: would this solution work in a different city? Why or why not?

LESSON 18

PQE: Mapping Access to Safe Water

Learning intention: we are learning to apply the PQE method to global maps of water-related disease and safe water access.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Recap: what do P, Q and E stand for?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Model reading a choropleth map of deaths from unsafe water sources.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Apply PQE to a map of deaths from unsafe water and a map of cholera or typhoid risk.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Which region appears most affected on today's maps, and by how much (quantify)?

Main task instructions

  1. Write a full PQE statement for the 'deaths from unsafe water sources' map.
  2. Write a second PQE statement for the cholera or typhoid risk map.
  3. Compare the two maps: do the same regions appear in both? What might explain that?
