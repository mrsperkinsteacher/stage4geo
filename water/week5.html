Week 5 — Water Scarcity

Syllabus focus: Water in the World. Outcomes: GE4-PER-01, GE4-MAN-01. Thread: Seeking Justice.

Week at a glance: L13 explains the causes of water scarcity; L14 examines Cape Town's Day Zero crisis; L15 studies Singapore's water management strategies.
LESSON 13

Causes of Water Scarcity

Learning intention: we are learning the six reasons water availability varies, and the difference between physical and economic water scarcity.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think 'water scarcity' means? Write your best guess.
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Teach latitude, altitude, ocean currents, distance from the sea, population and topography as the six reasons for scarcity, and define physical vs economic water scarcity.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Isaiah 41:17–18 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Match each of the six reasons to a real-world example, then classify given scenarios as physical or economic scarcity.
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Give one example of economic water scarcity (water exists, but people can't access it).

Main task instructions

  1. Copy the definitions of physical and economic water scarcity.
  2. For each of the six reasons for scarcity, write a one-sentence example.
  3. Classify five given country scenarios as physical or economic water scarcity, justifying each choice.

Christian worldview — Seeking Justice

“The poor and needy search for water, but there is none… I will make rivers flow on barren heights.”

Isaiah 41:17–18

God notices when people search for water and find none; consider what it looks like for governments — and for us — to respond to that same need.

LESSON 14

Case Study: Cape Town's Day Zero

Learning intention: we are learning how drought, water wastage and population growth combined to bring Cape Town close to running out of water.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think a city would do if it was about to run out of water completely?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Case study: the 2017–2018 Cape Town water crisis and 'Day Zero', and how Stage 7 restrictions plus rain ended it.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Complete comprehension questions and a cause-effect scaffold on the Cape Town crisis.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): What was the single biggest cause of Cape Town's Day Zero crisis?

Main task instructions

  1. Read the Cape Town Day Zero case study summary.
  2. Complete the cause–effect scaffold linking drought, wastage and population growth to Day Zero.
  3. Answer the comprehension questions on how the crisis was eventually avoided.

LESSON 15

Case Study: Singapore's Solutions

Learning intention: we are learning how Singapore manages water scarcity through its 'Four National Taps' strategy.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): If your town had almost no natural water supply, what would you do?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Case study: Singapore's four water sources — imported water, NEWater (recycled), local catchment collection, and desalination.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Complete a PMI (Plus, Minus, Interesting) chart evaluating Singapore's strategies, then build a comparison table: Cape Town (crisis) vs Singapore (solutions).
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Which of Singapore's four strategies do you think is most sustainable long-term? Why?

Main task instructions

  1. Read the summary of Singapore's Four National Taps.
  2. Complete a PMI chart for each of the four strategies.
  3. Fill in a comparison table contrasting Cape Town's crisis response with Singapore's planned strategies.
