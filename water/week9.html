Week 9 — Water Hazards — the 2022 Lismore Floods

Syllabus focus: Water in the World. Outcomes: GE4-PRI-01, GE4-MAN-01. Thread: Building Community.

Week at a glance: L25 examines the causes of the 2022 Northern Rivers floods; L26 looks at impacts and responses; L27 links the event to climate change and flood management strategies.
LESSON 25

Causes of the 2022 Floods

Learning intention: we are learning what caused the record flooding in Lismore and the Northern Rivers in 2022.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you already know about the 2022 floods in Northern NSW?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Teach the record rainfall event and the catchment/topographic factors that made flooding so severe; briefly compare with historic Australian floods (Gundagai 1852, Brisbane 1974/2011, Hawkesbury–Georges River 1986).
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Summarise the causes of the 2022 floods and compare them with one historic Australian flood.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one factor that made the 2022 Lismore flood so severe.

Main task instructions

  1. Read the summary of the 2022 Northern Rivers/Lismore flood event.
  2. List the rainfall and catchment/topographic factors that contributed to its severity.
  3. Compare the 2022 flood with one historic Australian flood using a simple table.

LESSON 26

Impacts and Responses

Learning intention: we are learning the social, economic and environmental impacts of the 2022 floods, and how communities and governments responded.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Who do you think responds first when a flood like this happens?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Examine the impacts on Lismore and the response by individuals, the SES and government, including the 2022 flood inquiry recommendations.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Psalm 46:1–3 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Complete a cause–impact–response table for the 2022 floods.
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one recommendation from the flood inquiry aimed at reducing future impacts.

Main task instructions

  1. Read about the social, economic and environmental impacts of the floods on Lismore.
  2. Research the emergency response (SES, evacuation, relief) and government response (inquiry, recommendations).
  3. Complete a cause–impact–response table summarising what you've learned.

Christian worldview — Building Community

“God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble… though the mountains fall into the heart of the sea.”

Psalm 46:1–3

Communities facing flood do not face it alone; think about how neighbours, emergency services and faith communities become a refuge for each other in a crisis.

LESSON 27

Climate Change and Future Flood Risk

Learning intention: we are learning how climate change may affect future flood risk, and how flood impacts can be managed.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Do you think floods like this will become more or less common? Why?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Discuss climate change predictions for rainfall intensity and flood risk, and evaluate flood management strategies (levees, mitigation infrastructure, planning controls, house-raising/buy-back schemes).
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Complete the flood case study report, evaluating which management strategies would most reduce future risk for Lismore.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Which flood management strategy do you think is most effective, and why?

Main task instructions

  1. Read the summary of climate change predictions for rainfall intensity and flooding.
  2. Compare the four management strategies (levees, mitigation infrastructure, planning controls, buy-back schemes).
  3. Complete your flood case study report with a recommendation and justification.
