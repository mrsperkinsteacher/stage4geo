Week 10 — Revision and Unit Close (Flex)

Syllabus focus: Water in the World. Outcomes: GE4-COM-01. Thread: Building Community.

Week at a glance: L28 revises the whole unit for the Yearly Examination; L29 is a gallery walk, reflection and program evaluation, with time built in for catch-up.
LESSON 28

Exam Revision

Learning intention: we are learning to revise the Water in the World unit for the Yearly Examination.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Without notes, list five key terms from this unit.
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Structured revision of the whole unit: spatial distribution, the water cycle, scarcity, contamination, value of water, and the flood case study.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Practice data questions (climate graphs, PQE map tasks) and one practice extended response.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Which topic from this unit do you still feel least confident about?

Main task instructions

  1. Complete the practice climate graph and PQE questions in the revision booklet.
  2. Attempt the practice extended response question.
  3. Mark your own response against the model answer and note one thing to improve.

LESSON 29

Gallery, Reflection and Unit Close

Learning intention: we are learning to reflect on what we've learned and how our understanding — and our habits — have changed.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): 'I used to think... now I think...' — complete this sentence about water.
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Display catchment diagrams, PQE analyses and water audit findings around the room.
  3. Worldview (5 min): 1 Thessalonians 5:18 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Gallery walk of the term's work, followed by a written reflection and capacity matrix self-assessment.
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): One way my water use has changed this term is...

Main task instructions

  1. Walk the gallery of displayed work from the unit and note one thing that stands out.
  2. Complete the 'I used to think... now I think...' reflection.
  3. Complete the capacity matrix self-assessment (information → knowledge → know-how → wisdom).

Christian worldview — Building Community

“Give thanks in all circumstances.”

1 Thessalonians 5:18

A fitting close to a unit about a resource we too easily take for granted — gratitude for water, and for the people God uses to bring it to us.
