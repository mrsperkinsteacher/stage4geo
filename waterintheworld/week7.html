LESSON 18

Short vs Long-term Strategies

Learning intention: we are learning to classify water scarcity responses as short-term or long-term.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Is trucking in water a short-term or long-term solution? Why?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce a range of scarcity management strategies.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Classify a range of management strategies as short-term or long-term responses, with justification. (Worksheet 7.1)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one strategy you classified as long-term, and why.

Main task instructions

  1. Read through the list of water scarcity management strategies.
  2. Classify each as short-term or long-term.
  3. Justify two of your classifications in full sentences.

LESSON 19

Desalination Inquiry

Learning intention: we are learning to evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of desalination as a water scarcity response.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think 'desalination' means, based on the word itself?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Explain the desalination process and where it's used.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of desalination as a water scarcity response (environmental, economic, social). (Worksheet 7.2)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Is desalination, on balance, a good long-term solution? Justify in one sentence.

Main task instructions

  1. Read the summary of how desalination works.
  2. List its advantages and disadvantages across environmental, economic and social categories.
  3. Write a judgement on whether desalination is a sustainable long-term strategy.

LESSON 20

Access Data and Ranking Synthesis

Learning intention: we are learning to graph global access to water and rank scarcity responses using multiple criteria.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What criteria matter most when judging a solution: cost, fairness, or speed?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce the ranking criteria: cost, sustainability, equity and long-term impact.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Amos 5:24 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Graph global access to improved water sources; rank scarcity responses most-to-least effective using cost, sustainability, equity and long-term impact as criteria. (Worksheet 7.3)
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Which response did you rank highest, and which criterion mattered most in your decision?

Main task instructions

  1. Construct the graph of global access to improved water sources from the data provided.
  2. Rank the scarcity responses studied this week from most to least effective.
  3. Justify your top-ranked response using at least two of the four criteria.

Christian worldview — Seeking Justice

“Let justice roll on like a river, righteousness like a never-failing stream.”

Amos 5:24

Evaluating which response is fairest, not just cheapest or fastest, is itself an act of seeking justice.
