Stage 4 Geography | Year 8

Water in the World

An 11-week unit on water as a resource: its distribution, the water cycle and catchments, factors affecting availability, its value, scarcity and management, Aboriginal water Country custodianship, sustainable catchment management, and hydrological/atmospheric hazards (Cyclone Debbie, 2017).

Source: built around the GTA NSW & ACT "Water in the World" Stage 4 Resource (2026) — aligned to the NESA Geography 7–10 Syllabus (2024). Each week links its own worksheet(s) and PPT slides below. Teacher-only: Answer sheets (Worksheets 3.1, 5.1, 7.1).

Water in the World examines water as a renewable resource: how it moves through the environment, why it is so unevenly available, how it is valued, how scarcity is caused and managed, how Aboriginal Peoples care for water Country, and what happens when a hydrological or atmospheric hazard strikes — using Cyclone Debbie (2017) as an in-depth case study.

Why does the spatial distribution of water resources vary globally and within countries — and whose job is it to do something about it?
Week 1

Global Distribution of Water Resources

L1 is a diagnostic on where the world's water actually is; L2 builds a sector graph and bar graph of global water types; L3 applies the PQE method to a world map of water access.

Flood Risk, Relief Mapping and Fieldwork

L7 continues relief mapping to predict flood risk; L8 uses the NSW Flood Imagery Viewer to compare before/after flood imagery; L9 is a fieldwork investigation comparing runoff and infiltration at two local sites.

Week 4

Factors Influencing Water Availability and Climate Graphs

L10 identifies physical and human factors affecting water access; L11 constructs climate graphs for Cairns and Alice Springs; L12 uses an ArcGIS StoryMap to investigate river movement through catchments.

Open Week 4
Week 5

The Value of Water

L13 rotates through four stations on the economic, cultural, spiritual and aesthetic value of water; L14 is a personal reflection on how water is valued in the student's own community.

Week 6

Causes and Impacts of Water Scarcity

L15 matches the causes of water scarcity to examples; L16 is a student-selected case study on scarcity's impacts; L17 looks at remote Australian communities and media literacy through a political cartoon.

Responses to Water Scarcity

L18 classifies strategies as short vs long-term; L19 evaluates desalination; L20 graphs global access to water and ranks scarcity responses.

Week 9
