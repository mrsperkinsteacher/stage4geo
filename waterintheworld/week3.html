LESSON 7

Relief, Slope and Runoff

Learning intention: we are learning to calculate relief and use slope to predict runoff speed and flood risk.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Recap: what does it mean if contour lines are close together?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Teach how to calculate local relief between two points and link slope to runoff speed.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Calculate local relief between a hilltop and a river on the topographic map; identify steep vs gentle slopes; predict runoff speed/flood risk, justified by slope and relief.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Would a steeper slope increase or decrease flood risk downstream, and why?

Main task instructions

  1. Calculate the relief (height difference) between the two marked points on the map.
  2. Identify whether the slope between them is steep or gentle from the contour spacing.
  3. Predict the runoff speed and flood risk for this catchment, justifying your answer with the relief and slope data.

LESSON 8

NSW Flood Imagery Viewer

Learning intention: we are learning to compare before/after flood imagery and judge the effect of human settlement on flood severity.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think a suburb looks like from above during a major flood?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce the NSW Flood Imagery Viewer and the Linescan Flood Imagery 2021 layer, including the before/after slidebar.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Three-tier task: describe a flood's extent; explain its social/economic/environmental impacts; judge the extent to which human settlement affects flood severity, using a screenshot as evidence.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one piece of evidence from today's imagery that supports your judgement.

Main task instructions

  1. Open the NSW Flood Imagery Viewer and select the Linescan Flood Imagery 2021 layer.
  2. Use the before/after slidebar to describe the extent of one flood event.
  3. Complete the differentiated task matching your tier: describe, explain, or judge (with screenshot evidence).

LESSON 9

FIELDWORK: Infiltration and Runoff

Learning intention: we are learning to collect real fieldwork data comparing infiltration and runoff at two different sites.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Guess: would water soak in faster on grass or on concrete?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Explain the fieldwork task: comparing two contrasting local sites for infiltration and runoff risk.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Genesis 9:13 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): At two contrasting sites (e.g. grassed area vs paved area), measure infiltration with a simple pour-and-observe test, estimate runoff potential, record pollutant evidence, map flow paths to the nearest drain, and judge which site poses the greater catchment risk. (Worksheet 9.1 style fieldwork)
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Which of your two sites poses the greater catchment risk, and why?

Main task instructions

  1. At each site, pour a measured amount of water and time how long it takes to soak in (or note that it doesn't).
  2. Record any visible pollutants (litter, oil, fertiliser, leaf matter) near each site.
  3. Map the path water would take from each site to the nearest stormwater drain, then judge which site is the greater catchment risk.
