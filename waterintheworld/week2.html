Week 2 — The Water Cycle, Catchments and Topographic Mapping

Week at a glance: L4 revises the water cycle and catchment processes; L5 teaches contour lines and relief using the NSW topographic map viewer; L6 traces raindrops through different environments using the Global River Runner tool.
LESSON 4

The Water Cycle and Catchments

Learning intention: we are learning the stages of the water cycle and how catchment processes move water through the landscape.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Draw the water cycle from memory in 90 seconds.
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Revise evaporation, condensation, precipitation, infiltration and runoff, and how they operate within a catchment.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Psalm 24:1 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Label a water cycle diagram and explain one human impact on catchment processes. (Worksheet 2.1)
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one human activity that changes how water moves through a catchment.

Main task instructions

  1. Label each stage of the water cycle diagram provided.
  2. Under each label, note whether infiltration or runoff is occurring.
  3. Explain one way human activity (e.g. paving, land clearing) changes catchment processes.

Christian worldview — Caretaking Earth

“The earth is the Lord’s, and everything in it.”

Psalm 24:1

Catchments and rivers belong to God before they belong to any water authority.

LESSON 5

Contour Lines and Relief

Learning intention: we are learning to read contour lines and relief on a topographic map, and explain how they affect water flow.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think the lines on a topographic map actually represent?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Explicit teaching of contour lines and altitude using the NSW Spatial Services topographic map viewer over a river valley/catchment.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Identify contours on the map, determine altitude at given points, describe elevation change, and explain how relief affects water flow.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): If contour lines are close together, is the slope steep or gentle?

Main task instructions

  1. Open the NSW topographic map viewer and locate the river valley provided.
  2. Identify the altitude at three marked points using the contour lines.
  3. Describe how the change in elevation between two points would affect water flow and speed.

LESSON 6

Movement of a Raindrop

Learning intention: we are learning how topography, vegetation and landforms affect the movement of water using a digital tool.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): If you dropped a marble on the roof of your house, where would it end up?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce the Global River Runner tool and how to trace a raindrop's journey.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Use the Global River Runner tool to trace raindrops through two different environments and compare how topography, vegetation and landforms affect their movement. (Worksheet 2.2)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Which of your two raindrops reached the ocean faster, and why?

Main task instructions

  1. Drop a virtual raindrop in your first chosen location using Global River Runner.
  2. Record the path, distance and landforms it passes through.
  3. Repeat for a second, different location and compare the two journeys.
