LESSON 24

Sustainable Water Supply Video

Learning intention: we are learning about real strategies used to manage catchments sustainably.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think a water company has to plan for, beyond just 'having enough water'?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Watch "Telstra: Ensuring a sustainable water supply."
  3. Worldview (5 min): Deuteronomy 8:7 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Three-tier task: describe one catchment-management strategy from the video, explain its economic/social importance, then predict and justify future challenges.
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one future challenge to sustainable catchment management you predicted.

Main task instructions

  1. Watch the video and note the catchment-management strategy described.
  2. Explain its economic and social importance in a paragraph.
  3. Predict one future challenge to this strategy and justify your prediction.

LESSON 25

FIELDWORK: School Catchment Walk

Learning intention: we are learning to observe and map how rainwater moves through our own school catchment.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Guess: where does rainwater from our school oval actually end up?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Explain the fieldwork task and safety expectations for walking the school grounds.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Walk the school grounds to observe rainwater movement, identify potential pollutants, map runoff paths and drains, and predict effects on the wider catchment/downstream waterways. (Worksheet 9.1)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one pollutant you identified and where it would end up.

Main task instructions

  1. Walk the designated route, observing where rainwater collects and flows.
  2. Identify and record any potential pollutants near drains or runoff paths.
  3. Map the runoff paths to the nearest drain and predict where this water ends up downstream.

LESSON 26

Applied Sustainability Proposal

Learning intention: we are learning to propose a realistic strategy to improve catchment sustainability at our school.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What's one small change that could reduce pollution entering our school's drains?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Review the fieldwork findings from Lesson 25 as a class.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Propose one strategy to improve catchment sustainability at school or locally, explaining its pollution-reduction/water-quality benefits.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): State your proposed strategy in one sentence.

Main task instructions

  1. Using your fieldwork findings, identify the school's biggest catchment risk.
  2. Propose one realistic strategy to address it.
  3. Explain how your strategy would improve pollution reduction or water quality.
