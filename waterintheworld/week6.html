Week 6 — Causes and Impacts of Water Scarcity

Syllabus focus: Water in the World. Outcomes: GE4-PER-01, GE4-PRI-01, GE4-DFC-01. Thread: Seeking Justice.

Source: See iLearn/Teacher Resources Folder for PPT slides 36–46 · See iLearn/Teacher Resources Folder for Worksheets 5.1, 5.2, 6.1, 6.2, 6.3
Week at a glance: L15 matches the causes of water scarcity to examples; L16 is a student-selected case study on scarcity's impacts; L17 looks at remote Australian communities and media literacy through a political cartoon.
LESSON 15

Causes of Water Scarcity

Learning intention: we are learning the main causes of water scarcity and how declining water quality makes it worse.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What's the difference between there being 'no water' and there being water no one can safely use?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Teach the eight main causes of water scarcity, and the difference between physical and economic scarcity.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Isaiah 41:17–18 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Match the eight main causes of water scarcity to their definitions and a real-world example; interpret a cartoon on water pollution and declining water quality. (Worksheets 5.1, 5.2)
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one cause of water scarcity that is about quality, not quantity.

Main task instructions

  1. Match each of the eight causes to its correct definition.
  2. For each cause, add one real-world example.
  3. Interpret the water quality cartoon and explain its message in one sentence.

Christian worldview — Seeking Justice

“The poor and needy search for water, but there is none… I will make rivers flow on barren heights.”

Isaiah 41:17–18

God notices when people search for water and find none.

LESSON 16

Case Study Research

Learning intention: we are learning to research the environmental, social, economic and political impacts of water scarcity in a country of our choice.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Which country would you guess faces the most severe water scarcity today?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Model how to structure a case study covering four impact categories.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Student-selected case study researching the environmental, social, economic and political impacts of water scarcity, and judging possible future solutions. (Worksheet 6.1)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name the country you chose and one impact category you found most severe.

Main task instructions

  1. Choose a water-scarce country to research.
  2. Research and record its environmental, social, economic and political impacts of scarcity.
  3. Judge and justify one possible future solution for your chosen country.

LESSON 17

Remote Communities and Media Literacy

Learning intention: we are learning about water quality challenges in remote Australian communities, and how cartoons convey a viewpoint.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think 'bore water' is, and where might it be used?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Read about bore water use and water quality issues in remote Australian communities.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Complete the reading on remote-community water quality; deconstruct a political cartoon on water scarcity, identifying visual techniques and the viewpoint it conveys. (Worksheets 6.2, 6.3)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one visual technique the cartoonist used to convey their viewpoint.

Main task instructions

  1. Read the article on bore water and remote community water quality, and answer the comprehension questions.
  2. Study the political cartoon and list the visual techniques used.
  3. Explain, in your own words, the viewpoint the cartoon is conveying.
