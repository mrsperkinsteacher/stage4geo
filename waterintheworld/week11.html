LESSON 30

Cyclone Debbie Case Study

Learning intention: we are learning the causes and impacts of Cyclone Debbie (2017) as our in-depth hazard case study.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you already know about Cyclone Debbie, if anything?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce Cyclone Debbie (2017): its causes and its hydrological, environmental, social and economic impacts across Queensland and northern NSW.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Read the Cyclone Debbie case study summary and take structured notes on its causes and impacts by category.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one impact category Cyclone Debbie affected.

Main task instructions

  1. Read the Cyclone Debbie (2017) case study summary.
  2. Take notes under four headings: hydrological, environmental, social, economic.
  3. Discuss as a class: which category do you expect to be hardest to recover from?

LESSON 31

FORMAL ASSESSMENT: Cyclone Debbie Impact Ranking

Learning intention: we are demonstrating our ability to evaluate and rank hazard impacts using defined criteria.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Recap: what are the four ranking criteria we'll use today?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Recap severity, extent, duration and difficulty of recovery as ranking criteria.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): FORMAL ASSESSMENT: analyse the provided impact cards on Cyclone Debbie's hydrological, environmental, social and economic effects; rank them using severity, extent, duration and difficulty of recovery; justify which impact was most significant. (Worksheet 12.1)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Submit your completed ranking and justification.

Main task instructions

  1. Read each impact card carefully.
  2. Rank the impacts using all four criteria: severity, extent, duration, difficulty of recovery.
  3. Write your justification for which impact was most significant, using geographical terminology.

LESSON 32

Gallery Walk and Unit Close

Learning intention: we are learning to reflect on what we've learned this term and how our understanding has changed.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): 'I used to think... now I think...' — complete this sentence about water.
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Display graphs, maps and fieldwork findings from across the term.
  3. Worldview (5 min): 1 Thessalonians 5:18 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Gallery walk of the term's work, followed by a written reflection and capacity matrix self-assessment.
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): One way my understanding of water changed this term is...

Main task instructions

  1. Walk the gallery of displayed work from the unit and note one thing that stands out.
  2. Complete the 'I used to think... now I think...' reflection.
  3. Complete the capacity matrix self-assessment.
