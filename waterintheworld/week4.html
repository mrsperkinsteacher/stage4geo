LESSON 10

Factors Influencing Water Availability

Learning intention: we are learning to identify the physical and human factors that affect water flows and access in different places.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): List two reasons a town might struggle to access water, beyond 'not enough rain'.
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Teach physical factors (climate, geology, topography) and human factors (infrastructure, demand, land use, governance).
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Scenario-based task: identify the physical and human factors affecting water flows and access across 8 given scenarios. (Worksheet 3.1)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one physical and one human factor from today's scenarios.

Main task instructions

  1. Read each of the 8 scenarios provided.
  2. Identify whether each scenario's main issue is a physical or human factor (or both).
  3. Justify your classification for two scenarios of your choice.

LESSON 11

Constructing Climate Graphs: Cairns vs Alice Springs

Learning intention: we are learning to construct and compare climate graphs for two contrasting Australian cities.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Guess: which city gets more rain — Cairns or Alice Springs?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Recap climate graph construction: temperature as a line, precipitation as bars.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Proverbs 25:2 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Construct dual climate graphs for Cairns and Alice Springs; compare rainfall and temperature patterns to explain their differing water-access challenges. (Worksheet 3.2)
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Which city's water challenge is about too little rain, and which is about seasonal extremes?

Main task instructions

  1. Construct a climate graph for Cairns using the data provided.
  2. Construct a climate graph for Alice Springs using the data provided.
  3. Compare the two graphs and explain how each city's climate creates a different water-access challenge.

LESSON 12

River Movement — ArcGIS StoryMap

Learning intention: we are learning to investigate how a river moves through a catchment using an interactive story map.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think changes about a river between its source and its mouth?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce the ArcGIS StoryMap tool and how to navigate it.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Use the ArcGIS StoryMap to investigate river movement through a catchment: calculate distances, assess topography's impact, and describe water movement at the river mouth.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name one way topography changed the river's behaviour along its course.

Main task instructions

  1. Work through the ArcGIS StoryMap from the river's source to its mouth.
  2. Record the approximate distance travelled and any major topography changes noted.
  3. Describe how the river's behaviour changes as it nears its mouth.
