Week 5 — The Value of Water

Syllabus focus: Water in the World. Outcomes: GE4-TAP-01, GE4-PER-01. Thread: Embracing Diversity.

Source: PPT slides 32–34 · Worksheets 4.1, 4.2 (GTA NSW & ACT "Water in the World" Stage 4 Resource, 2026)
Week at a glance: L13 rotates through four stations on the economic, cultural, spiritual and aesthetic value of water; L14 is a personal reflection on how water is valued in the student's own community.
LESSON 13

Values Gallery Walk

Learning intention: we are learning the four ways water can be valued: economic, cultural, spiritual and aesthetic.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Besides drinking it, why else might water matter to a person or community?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce the four value categories with examples of each.
  3. Worldview (5 min): John 4:13–14 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Rotate through four stations (economic, cultural, spiritual, aesthetic value of water) and complete the reflection table at each. (Worksheet 4.1)
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Which station's example surprised you most, and why?

Main task instructions

  1. Visit each of the four value stations in your group.
  2. At each station, record one example and complete the reflection question.
  3. Compare your table with a partner's when you finish.

Christian worldview — Embracing Diversity

“Whoever drinks the water I give them will never thirst.”

John 4:13–14

Jesus meets the woman at the well in her literal daily need for water before pointing to something deeper.

LESSON 14

Value of Water to Me and My Community

Learning intention: we are learning to reflect on how water is valued in our own local context.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): Name one place near you where water is valued for more than just drinking.
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Discuss how the four value categories apply locally.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Personal, place-based reflection on how water is valued in the student's own local context. (Worksheet 4.2)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): One sentence: water matters to my community because...

Main task instructions

  1. Think of a local place where water plays an important role (a beach, river, pool, dam).
  2. Complete the four-quadrant reflection for that place (economic/cultural/spiritual/aesthetic).
  3. Share one reflection with the class.
