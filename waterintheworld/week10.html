LESSON 27

Synoptic Charts

Learning intention: we are learning to interpret synoptic weather charts, including pressure systems and fronts.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What do you think the circles and lines on a weather map actually mean?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Explicit teaching of pressure systems, wind patterns and weather symbols.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Interpret synoptic charts (isobars, highs/lows, fronts, troughs) using the Bureau of Meteorology's 4-day forecast sheet. (Worksheet 10.1)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Is a 'low' pressure system generally associated with fine or wet weather?

Main task instructions

  1. Study the synoptic chart symbol key provided.
  2. Using the BOM 4-day forecast sheet, describe the weather for two different cities on two different days.
  3. Explain how the forecast changes over the four days and why.

LESSON 28

Cyclone Formation and Impacts

Learning intention: we are learning how cyclones form and researching their impacts.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What ingredients do you think a cyclone needs to form?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Watch "Learn Bright: Hurricanes" and discuss cyclone formation.
  3. Main task (20–25 min): Draw a diagram of cyclone/hurricane formation; research and create a poster on one cyclone impact (flooding, storm surge, wind damage); investigate a hurricane's path using an ArcGIS StoryMap. (Worksheet 10.2)
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): Name the impact category you chose for your poster.

Main task instructions

  1. Draw and label a diagram showing how a cyclone forms.
  2. Choose one impact category (flooding, storm surge, or wind damage) and research it.
  3. Create a poster summarising your chosen impact, then explore the hurricane path StoryMap.

External links

  • Learn Bright: Hurricanes
  • ArcGIS StoryMaps – hurricane path

LESSON 29

Frequency, Extent and Severity

Learning intention: we are learning to classify hazards using frequency, extent and severity, and to explain climate change's role.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): What's the difference between a hazard being 'frequent' and being 'severe'?
  2. Teach (10–15 min): Introduce the frequency/extent/severity framework and apply it to example hazards.
  3. Worldview (5 min): Psalm 46:1–3 discussion (see box below).
  4. Main task (20–25 min): Classify example hazards against the frequency/extent/severity framework; read how climate change increases cyclone frequency and severity, and write a PEEL-scaffolded paragraph response. (Worksheet 11.1)
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): Explain, in one sentence, how climate change can increase a hazard's severity.

Main task instructions

  1. Classify the three example hazards provided using frequency, extent and severity.
  2. Read the article on climate change and cyclone frequency/severity.
  3. Write a PEEL paragraph explaining how ONE human-induced factor can increase the frequency and severity of atmospheric hazards.

Christian worldview — Building Community

Psalm 46:1–3
